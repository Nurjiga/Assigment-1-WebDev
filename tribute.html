About Albert Einstein

Albert Einstein (14 March 1879 – 18 April 1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who is best known for developing the theory of relativity. Einstein also made important contributions to quantum theory. His mass–energy equivalence formula E = mc2, which arises from special relativity, has been called "the world's most famous equation". He received the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics for ‹See Tfd›his services to theoretical physics, and especially for his discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect.

Major Events

  1. 1905 – Annus Mirabilis (Miracle Year): Published four groundbreaking papers, including the special theory of relativity, revolutionizing physics.
  2. 1921 – Nobel Prize in Physics: Awarded for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, which laid the foundation for quantum theory.
  3. 1933 – Emigration to the U.S.: Left Nazi Germany and joined Princeton University, where he worked at the Institute for Advanced Study.
  4. 1939 – Letter to Roosevelt: Co-signed a letter warning about nuclear weapons, which influenced the Manhattan Project.

Inventions

  • 1905 – Theory of Special Relativity
  • 1905 – Photoelectric Effect Explanation
  • 1915 – Theory of General Relativity
  • 1924 – Bose–Einstein Condensate (with Satyendra Nath Bose)
